About Me

I am an Environmental Scientist working across research, environmental consulting, and ecological fieldwork in the UAE and Qatar.

My work focuses on understanding environmental systems through field observation, laboratory analysis, and data-driven approaches — translating ecological complexity into structured insights that support real-world decision-making.

Biography

I am Turkish, born and raised in Abu Dhabi, and shaped by a multicultural background that has influenced both how I see the world and how I approach environmental science. Growing up along the Arabian Gulf, I developed an early fascination with marine systems and the complex dynamics that sustain life within them.

This environment led me to explore how ecosystems function under extreme conditions, and how marine, terrestrial, and climate systems interact within rapidly developing regions. Over time, this curiosity evolved into a broader interest in sustainability, environmental systems, and the role of science in understanding and addressing global challenges.

What Drives Me

I am driven by a fundamental interest in understanding systems — not only individual components, but the relationships that define ecosystems.

My work is motivated by questions of resilience: how organisms and environments adapt under stress, and how these insights can inform sustainability, climate resilience, and environmental decision-making.

How I Work

My approach integrates fieldwork, laboratory analysis, and data interpretation — moving from observation to application.

I translate environmental data into structured insights that support environmental assessments, regulatory processes, and sustainability strategies.
